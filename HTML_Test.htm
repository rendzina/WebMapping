First but decidedly not by any means last ...
Tags can be in upper or lower case! It really doesn't matter a jot.

Note that <u> used to mean underline, and many browsers still interpret like this. HTML5 redefines the u tag for text that should be stylistically different from normal text, such as misspelled words or proper nouns in Chinese. Generally avoid underlining as it can be confused for a link. By the way, lines can be as long as you want and the browser wraps text within the defined window of division.

But unlike in a word processor, you must still doo your own speeeling! Better still use an editor or IDE like NetBeans or Notepad++.
